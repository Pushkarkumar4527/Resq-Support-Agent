1. What is the standard timeline for processing a refund?
Refunds for canceled subscriptions or billing errors are typically processed within 5-7 business days from the date of approval. The funds will be returned to the original payment method used.

2. How can I reset my account password?
To reset your password, click on the "Forgot Password" link on the login page. You will receive an email with a secure link to create a new password. The link expires in 24 hours.

3. Are there any API rate limits for the Enterprise plan?
Yes, the Enterprise plan includes a standard API rate limit of 10,000 requests per minute per tenant. If you require higher throughput, please contact your dedicated account manager.

4. Can I change my billing cycle from monthly to annually?
Absolutely. You can switch to an annual billing cycle at any time from your Account Settings under the "Billing" tab. The change will take effect immediately, and you will be billed for a prorated amount for the remainder of the year.

5. What payment methods are accepted for B2B SaaS subscriptions?
We accept all major credit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express), PayPal, and bank wire transfers (ACH/SEPA) for annual contracts exceeding $5,000.

6. How do I add or remove user seats from my plan?
Workspace admins can manage user seats from the "Team Management" dashboard. Added seats are billed immediately at a prorated rate, while removed seats will result in a credit applied to your next billing cycle.

7. What is your Service Level Agreement (SLA) for support response times?
Our standard SLA guarantees a response within 24 hours for basic plans, 4 hours for Pro plans, and 1 hour for Enterprise plans during standard business hours (9 AM - 5 PM EST).

8. Does ResQ offer a data export feature?
Yes, all users can export their account data in CSV or JSON format from the Data Management section in Settings. Enterprise users can also configure automated daily backups to their own Amazon S3 buckets.

9. How are overage charges calculated if we exceed our monthly storage limit?
If your workspace exceeds the allocated storage, you will be billed at a rate of $0.05 per GB for the excess storage at the end of the billing cycle. We will send a notification when you reach 80% of your limit.

10. What happens to our data if we cancel our subscription?
Upon cancellation, your data will be retained in a read-only state for 30 days, allowing you to reactivate or export it. After 30 days, all data is permanently deleted from our servers in compliance with GDPR.

11. Why am I getting an HTTP 429 "Too Many Requests" error?
An HTTP 429 error means your application has exceeded the API rate limit of 10,000 requests per minute per tenant. 
To resolve this, you must implement an "exponential backoff" strategy in your code. When a 429 error is returned, your system should pause before retrying the request. 
Start with a 1-second delay, and double the wait time for each subsequent failure (e.g., 1s, 2s, 4s, 8s). We also highly recommend adding "jitter" (a small amount of random time) to your backoff to prevent the thundering herd problem. You can check the "Retry-After" header in the API response for the exact number of seconds to wait before making a new request.

12. What if I cancel my annual Enterprise subscription halfway through the year?
For annual Enterprise contracts, we do not issue cash refunds for partial years. However, if you cancel before the 6th month of your billing cycle, a prorated credit will be applied to your account ledger. This credit can be used towards future reactivations or transferred to another tenant within your organization. Cancellations occurring after the 6-month mark forfeit the remaining balance.

13. Is ResQ SOC2 compliant and where is my data geographically stored?
Yes, ResQ is SOC2 Type II certified. We undergo annual third-party audits to ensure strict adherence to security and privacy frameworks. By default, all customer data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3) and is hosted in AWS US-East (N. Virginia). Enterprise customers can request localized data residency in EU-Central (Frankfurt) or AP-South (Mumbai) to comply with regional data sovereignty laws.

14. What should I do if my server is completely down and the ResQ dashboard won't load?
This is classified as a Severity 1 (Sev-1) Critical Outage. Do not wait for standard email support. Please explicitly ask this agent to "create a high-priority support ticket" immediately. Provide your User ID and the exact system behavior you are experiencing. This action will bypass the standard queue and immediately page our on-call Site Reliability Engineering (SRE) team.
